Design directions

Mockups for the marjoriebagley.com redesign — same content in each, so the comparison is purely visual. Open each; imagine living with it for five years. Round 2 additions: a violin-photo variant of the front-runner (5b) and three wider-net directions (6–8) after the observation that 1, 2, and 4 cluster around the same warm-paper-serif theme.

  1. Concert Program

    Burgundy on warm ivory with hairline gold rules; set like the title page of a printed program. The maroon-family direction — continuity with the current site.

  2. Ivory Editorial

    Near-monochrome literary elegance: small-caps masthead, Crimson body, one oxblood accent. The quietest direction; events as a restrained text list.

  3. Conservatory Modern

    The one sans-serif direction, steel blue and white, with the portrait photo and three role cards (Studio / Orchestra / Chamber). Closest to how peer institutions present faculty.

  4. Engraved Edition

    Garamond set like a Henle/Bärenreiter score title page, staff-line ornaments, dotted-leader season index. The most austere and most "classical music" of the five.

  5. Piedmont Dusk

    Deep spruce-green bands with warm cream and a terracotta accent; first-person introduction as the hero. The most distinctive palette while staying sober. Current front-runner.

    ↳ 5b · with the 1703 violin — variant working in the photo of Marjorie's actual instrument as a "printed plate" on the cream sheet, with caption.

  6. Nocturne

    The dark one: concert hall at night — warm charcoal-plum, a single amber "stage light" accent, big italic serif. Dramatic register, quiet execution.

  7. Folio

    A different layout paradigm: fixed left rail (name + nav) with content flowing beside it; cool slate-and-Prussian-blue palette; season as a fine table. Reads like a well-set book folio.

  8. Overture

    Color-blocked and asymmetric: indigo masthead band, ochre ticket-stub strip, numbered sections on an off-center grid. The most contemporary-editorial of the set — arts-organization energy at personal-site scale.

  9. Overture × Piedmont Dusk

    Requested hybrid: Overture's banded, numbered-section layout in Piedmont Dusk's spruce/cream/terracotta with Lora type. Three variants differing only in where the 1703 violin lives:

    ↳ 9a · in the header — a cream-matted print hanging in the masthead's empty right corner, under the credentials.

    ↳ 9b · at the foot — resting across the page at the bottom of the off-white area, just above the dark footer band.

    ↳ 9c · as the divider — the violin is the strip between the dark masthead and the paper content; the instrument becomes part of the page's architecture. (Claude's suggestion.)

Notes for review. The violin caption in 5b says "made in 1703" per Ian's recollection — verify the year (and the caption wording) with Marjorie before launch. Event listings show real 2026–27 GSO season titles, but which concerts (and any solo/recital entries, e.g. "Faculty Recital") are illustrative — no claim about what Marjorie is actually playing. All five follow the anti-staleness rules from the design research: no photo dependence (except the portrait in #3), no empty-able widgets, season framing, ≤5 nav items. Elements can be mixed — e.g. #4's staff lines in #1's palette, or #5's first-person hero in #2's typography.
